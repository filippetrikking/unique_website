About

Learn more about Mr. Filip Petrik, the visionary behind this site, and what makes this man unique.
I was born on 4 April 1996 in Malmö, Sweden.
Malmö and the neighbouring city of Lund are practically where I've grown up and spent my childhood and adulthood.
I was educated in Swedish elementary school and later in the Swedish high school of Cathedral School in Lund.
I am a former student at Lund University, where I studied Computer Science (programming and mathematics).
I am well-versed in university level mathematics.
Previously I worked as a substitute teacher throughout Skåne County.
I taught younger students and shared my knowledge in the schools of Scania.
I used to teach French, mathematics, Swedish, English and other subjects.
Since my teens, I have had French as favorite subject, and I speak fluently ever since my journey to Nice, France.
I'm curious by nature; I've studied Chinese, Japanese, Arabic, Spanish, German, and Thai throughout my years,
with some beginner skills in comprehension and speaking.
I created this website to document my journey throughout life and to be visible in the digital world.
I love life and I am passionate living, loving, transforming my skillset into joy for others.
I have a 2 DAN black belt in Shotokan Karate and thus I am passionate about karate and other martial arts.
In my spare time I like reading, programming, cooking, hiking and travelling.
I have made a lot of travels in my days with my father, brother and mother.
The yearly summer visits to Slovakia were formative of my younger years.
My father brought us during childhood, to great destinations such as Italy, Austria,
Tenerife, Norway, Thailand, Barbados and France.
Last year I made a journey over the entire world in only 4 1/2 months.
My nickname in Thailand was Nong Feel (Nong is 'younger brother').
I travelled to Thailand, Japan and Vietnam and encountered a completely different world.

About my journey to Thailand


Thailand is a marvelous place.
I left Copenhagen Airport on 24th of July 2025 in order to... well, what?
I didn't have anything specific in mind, but Lord knows I travelled out of an insatiable desire to backpack and to be an adventurer.
My first transit was in Istanbul, then Dubai Airport, and then Mumbai.
In Mumbai I fell ill due to sleeplessness and jetlag. I had to speak to a doctor at Mumbai Airport.
Everything was strange. I felt like the ground was moving under me, yet reasonably so, there was no earthquake.
Nevermind. I committed to the journey ahead and flew to Bangkok Suvarnabhumi Airport.
Suvarnabhumi Airport is gigantic. Tens of thousands of people are going from and to the Airport. It's located in the far east of Bangkok.
The other airport is called Don Mueang Airport (DMK). It has a very boring interior as opposed to SVB Airport.
My journey started with Cube Hostel Khaosan, a nice hostel close to Khaosan Road. 0463595357

My journey to Surat Thani and Koh Phangan

Nong Thun and Pi Su

My dear friends from Surat Thani were as welcoming as ever.

They both entertained their guests with charm and splendor.

Suppanika Wichianchiau is my very dear friend and a skilled business owner.

Her hostel, 2Street Hostel, is run by her and her cleaning lady Pi Ple.

I felt like a king here. Pi Su's sense of service is impeccable and there is always a friendly atmosphere here, with frequent visitors from all over the world.

Supannika's cooking is also impeccable.

We met in 2024, when I was in Thailand on my first solo-exploration journey to Thailand. Our common friend Maurice introduced us.

I'm very happy to call them my friends.

I can only recommend her hostel from the bottom of my heart.

Second journey to Koh Phangan


In Nana I fell ill in pneumonia. In a somewhat shady back alley, I lingered and groaned in my sickness. It wasn't easy, but at least I had a place to stay and so forth.
Hm. How do you describe this place to someone who is not familiar with Bangkok?
Nana Plaza is the biggest adult playground in Asia. Admittedly, I went there and fooled around a bit with a nice lady. She was a total sweetheart but after those two drinks, I never saw her again.
Admittedly, there was flirting on both sides.
Thai women are some of the most interesting people you will meet. Hot and cold temperaments all over the place!
Admittedly, I've dated 3 Thai women.
Later I went to Bangkok Christian Hospital. The service here was impeccable and I met a very nice doctor called Dr. Sompong.
I stepped out of my comfort zone and all of a sudden here am I, in a new world with lots and lots of fascinating people!
Sukhumvit. The best street in Bangkok. There is no place I'd rather be in life than Sukhumvit... and I'm not even joking.
